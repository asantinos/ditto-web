## How history works

Ditto can keep your **100 most recent successful transcriptions** in **Settings → History**. New entries appear first and include the final text, creation time, Whisper model, language setting, and whether smart formatting was applied.

History stores the text that Ditto actually delivered after optional Qwen formatting. Empty audio and recognized silence markers are never added.

## Working with an entry

From the History panel you can:

- **Copy** an entry back to the clipboard.
- **Delete** one entry without affecting the others.
- **Clear all** history entries at once.

Deleting an entry only removes Ditto's local copy. It cannot remove text that was already pasted into another app.

## Turn history off

History is enabled by default. Turn off **Save transcription history** in the History panel if you do not want future transcriptions stored. Existing entries remain available until you delete them or clear the history.

<Callout type="note">
History is local. It is stored in `%APPDATA%\ditto\ditto-history.json`, is never uploaded, and is removed by **Settings → Advanced → Delete all data**.
</Callout>

## Privacy details

Only final text and small metadata fields are saved. Audio is never part of the history: temporary WAV files are still deleted immediately after each transcription finishes.

See [Privacy & data](/docs/privacy-and-data) for the complete local-data breakdown.
